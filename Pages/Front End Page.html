Mental Health in the Technology Start-Up Industry

Project Overview


Mental health is a serious issue that is often hidden to others because of the stigmatism of a perceived weakness. The tech industry is no exception. It is full off start-ups where many people attempt to prove the long-term success of their ideas within a tight deadline. This pressure has caused the rate of mental illnesses among tech professionals to be more than double the rate of current US adults, according to a recent survey done by OSMI. The following project focuses on getting help for mental health concerns in the technology start-up workplace. This study is currently done in two parts with the first part analyzing data from a 2014 survey and the next part analyzing data from a 2016 survey.


Methodology


Both parts of the study was done in several sections. The first part was to create an audience profile of the total dataset, and then two seperate ones, one for each gender. This was done to create an baseline from which the next step, which was using model selection and logistic regression to select the most optimal factors given a specific method. Each profile was composed of the following: summary statistics for the age and distributions for the location, predominance of technology within the organization and whether they sought treatment.

Once the profiles were created, the study employed a backwards stepwise model to select the best factors to look at for sepcifc strategies for companies that wanted to improve accesibility to mental health solutions. Once the best models were chosen, a logistic regression was done to ensure the relaibility and set up predictions, which would be another test of relaibility.
